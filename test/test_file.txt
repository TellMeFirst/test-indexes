The Academy Awards, commonly known as The Oscars, is an annual American
awards ceremony honoring achievements in the film industry. Winners
are awarded the statuette, officially the Academy Award of Merit, that
is much better known by its nickname Oscar. The awards, first presented
in 1929 at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel , are overseen by the Academy
of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS). The Oscar Academy Awards
are an electoral race to the trophy to best film in any category. The
awards ceremony was first televised in 1953 and is now seen live in
more than 200 countries. The Oscars is also the oldest entertainment
awards ceremony; its equivalents, the Emmy Awards for television, the
Tony Awards for theatre, and the Grammy Awards for music and recording,
are modeled after the Academy Awards. The 86th Academy Awards ceremony
was held on March 2, 2014, at the Dolby Theatre in Los Angeles. History
